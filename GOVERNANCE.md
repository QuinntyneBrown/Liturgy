# Project Governance

## Principles

Liturgy is maintained openly, with decisions guided by correctness, maintainability, accessibility, security, and the project's stated purpose: helping teams build technology in the rhythm of the FaithTech Playbook. Technical convenience does not override these responsibilities. In particular, the integrity of the enforcement engine — that gates and 5R completion are decided on the server and cannot be bypassed by a client — is a core value, not an implementation detail.

## Roles

### Contributors

Anyone who submits an issue, design, documentation, code, review, or other project improvement is a contributor. Contributions must follow [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md) and the [Code of Conduct](CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

### Maintainers

Maintainers triage issues, review and merge changes, manage releases, moderate project spaces, and steward security and community standards. The repository owner, [Quinntyne Brown](https://github.com/QuinntyneBrown), is the current lead maintainer.

## Decision making

Day-to-day decisions are made through issues and pull requests. Maintainers seek rough consensus, using documented requirements, test evidence, and architecture decisions. The lead maintainer makes the final repository decision when consensus cannot be reached.

Significant technical or product decisions should be recorded in [docs/DECISIONS.md](docs/DECISIONS.md). Security-sensitive decisions may remain private.

## Becoming a maintainer

Maintainers may invite contributors who demonstrate sustained, constructive participation; sound technical judgment; reliable reviews; and consistent adherence to project values. Maintainer access may be removed for inactivity, security reasons, or violations of project policy after reasonable notice when circumstances permit.

## Changes to governance

Governance changes are proposed and reviewed through a pull request. Material changes should remain open long enough for active contributors to respond.
