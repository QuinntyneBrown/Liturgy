Why Liturgy

Your process forms your team. Choose one worth being formed by.

Most tools track work. Liturgy shapes it. Every project moves Discover → Discern → Develop → Demonstrate behind hard gates, and every card completes five movements — Request, Receive, Review, Render, Rejoice — before it can be called done. Prayer, discernment, and gratitude aren't reminders on a wiki page. They're the workflow.

"Whatever you do, work at it with all your heart, as working for the Lord." Colossians 3:23

01 · Hard gates

Gates you can't argue with.

A phase stays locked until the one before it is truly finished — artifacts complete, decision recorded, goal set. When a gate is blocked, Liturgy says so, names what's missing, and points at the door. No skipping to the build.

03 · The 5R loop

Every card completes the loop.

Request — invite the Spirit into the work. Receive — wait, and write down what comes. Review, Render, Rejoice. Five movements, logged one at a time on every work item, like canonical hours — a small liturgy repeated until the work is whole.

04 · Together

One workspace. Every project. Always current.

Run every initiative in one shared workspace — from the lament workshop in Discover to the gratitude wall in Demonstrate. When a teammate moves a card, logs a movement, or opens a gate, everyone sees it the moment it happens. No refresh, no stale board, no "who has the latest?"
