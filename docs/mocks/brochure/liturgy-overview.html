Why Liturgy

The framework is beautiful. Keeping it is hard.

Many teams adopt a redemptive framework with real conviction — and then watch it evaporate under sprint pressure. The discernment step gets skipped "just this once." The gratitude practice becomes a retro item nobody reads. Within a quarter, the Playbook is a poster on the wall and the team is back to shipping tickets.

Liturgy's answer is enforcement. The workspace itself holds the rhythm: a phase stays locked until the one before it is truly finished, a decision must be recorded before a single card is built, and no work item can be called done until all five movements of co-creation are logged. The tool doesn't remind your team of the process — it is the process.

Your process forms your team. Choose one worth being formed by.

See clearly

Choose wisely

Co-create

Hard gates between phases

Inside Develop

The 5R loop: a ritual inside every card.

Every work item runs the five movements of co-creation, logged one at a time — like canonical hours, a small liturgy repeated until the work is whole. Each card on the board carries its progress as five small pips, and Done stays locked until all five are logged.

  • 1Request
  • 2Receive
  • 3Review
  • 4Render
  • 5Rejoice
  • 1
    Request — invite the Spirit into the work

    Name what you're seeking before you start. A short, honest ask.

  • 2
    Receive — wait, and write down what comes

    Capture the ideas, nudges, and constraints that surface — without editing them yet.

  • 3
    Review — synthesize what emerged

    Shape the raw ideas into a direction the team can build.

  • 4
    Render — build toward what you saw

    Make it real. Link the work and note what the build taught you along the way.

  • 5
    Rejoice — give thanks for what was made

    Name the good in what came about, and who it serves. Rejoice closes the loop.
